Brown sugar tea

Brown Sugar: The key component of the drink, brown sugar is used to create the caramelized flavor that defines this beverage. It is typically cooked down until it melts and thickens into a syrupy consistency. Milk: Milk, often in the form of fresh milk or creamer, is added to create a creamy and smooth base for the tea. It helps balance the sweetness of the brown sugar and adds richness to the overall flavor. Tea Base: Brown Sugar Bubble Tea can be made with different tea bases, such as black tea, oolong tea, or even green tea. The tea is brewed and then combined with the other ingredients. Tapioca Pearls (Boba): Tapioca pearls are an essential component of bubble tea. These chewy spheres are typically boiled until they become soft and slightly gummy. They are then soaked in a brown sugar syrup to infuse them with additional flavor. Ice and Water: Ice and water are added to cool down the drink and make it refreshing.
